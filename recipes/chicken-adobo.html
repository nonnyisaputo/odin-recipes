Chicken Adobo

Description

The unofficial national dish of the Philippines. Tangy, salty, sweet, spicy. Braised chicken perfect with rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) canola oil
  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken legs, separated into thighs and drumsticks (about 2 1/2 pounds)
  • Kosher salt
  • 2 whole fresh bay leaves (or 3 whole dried bay leaves)
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons whole black peppercorns
  • 1 1/4 cups (300 ml) water
  • 1 cup (240 ml) soy sauce
  • 1 cup (240 ml) rice vinegar
  • Steamed white rice or garlic fried rice, for serving

Steps

  1. In heavy pot or Dutch oven, heat oil over medium heat until shimmering. Dry chicken with paper towels. Season lighly with salt.
  2. Sear chicken until brown (6 to 7 minutes). Do no overcrowd pan. Flip and cook for about 3 minutes. Transfer browned chicken to a plate and set aside.
  3. Add garlic, bay leaves, and peppercors to the pot. Cook until fragant and golden brown (about 30 seconds). Add water to deglaze pot. Use wooden spoon to scrape up the brown bits. Add soy sauce and vinegar. Return chicken to the pot. Increase heat to high. Bring to a boil and reduce to simmer. Cover with lid. Simmer until chicken is cooked and tender (20 minutes). Turn chicken pieces halfway through Turn chicken pieces halfway through.
  4. Serve immediately with rice. Tip: Even better after sitting overnight in the refrigerator and reheated.
